# Player Guide: Character Sheet

Use this sheet to build and track a Breath of the World character. Cloud Save starts automatic profile sync for your character.

## 1. Name And Level

- Enter your character name at the top.
- Set your level in the header.
- The Level box shows your remaining Feats, Skill Points, and Combat Points.
- If you lower your level, the sheet may reduce overspent feats or points to match the new limit.

## 2. Attributes

- Every fresh character starts with all attributes at `d4`.
- Choose a die for each attribute: `d4`, `d6`, `d8`, `d10`, `d12`, or `d20`.
- Attribute dice are trust-based. The sheet shows the current spread but does not enforce the starting cap.
- Attribute rows automatically sort from highest die to lowest die.

## 3. Feats

- Feats are chosen from the Feats box.
- Level 1 gives 1 feat.
- After Level 1, you gain 1 feat every other level.
- The Feats Left counter decreases when you add a feat.
- When you have no feat picks left, the feat picker locks until your level grants another pick or you remove a feat.

## 4. Mantle

- A Mantle represents your presence, demeanor, identity, and reputation.
- Choose one Mantle from the Mantle box.
- Mantles can grant bonuses to normal skills or combat skills.
- Mantle bonuses do not spend Skill Points or Combat Points.
- Skills improved by your Mantle are highlighted green and show a bonus badge.

## 5. Skills

- Spend Skill Points in the 5e Skills box.
- Level 1 gives 4 Skill Points.
- Each level after Level 1 gives 3 more Skill Points.
- Increasing a skill spends from Skill Points Left.
- If you try to spend too many, the sheet reduces the changed value back within your available pool.

## 6. Combat

- Open the Combat tab on the right side of the sheet.
- Current HP can be edited.
- Max HP is calculated from Constitution.
- Combat skills spend Combat Points.
- Level 1 starts with 0 Combat Points.
- Each level after Level 1 gives 1 Combat Point.
- Mantle bonuses to combat skills can exceed your normal Combat Point pool.

## 7. Notes And Inventory

- Use Notes for story details, reminders, relationships, injuries, or campaign information.
- Use Inventory for gear, coin, supplies, weapons, and keepsakes.

## 8. Character Image

- Use the menu button in the header to add or clear a character image.
- Hover the eye button to preview the character image without the sheet boxes in the way.
- The image is included in your cloud-synced character data.

## 9. Saving And Loading

- When the sheet opens, choose a cloud save or create a new character.
- Use Cloud Save once to create or attach a cloud profile; after that, edits sync to that profile automatically.
- Use Cloud Load to switch to another saved character.
- Use Reset to clear the sheet back to a fresh character.

## 10. Quick Character Creation Flow

1. Enter character name.
2. Set level to `1`.
3. Choose attribute dice.
4. Pick 1 feat.
5. Choose a Mantle.
6. Spend 4 Skill Points.
7. Add notes, inventory, and character image.
8. Save the character file.
